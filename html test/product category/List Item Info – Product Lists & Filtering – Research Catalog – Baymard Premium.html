List Item Info

This topic covers what information to include in list items and how to format and display it.

The purpose of list item info is to help users to easily and accurately determine which products to investigate further — essentially, whether the corresponding product page is worth visiting or if it should be skipped. Not only does the required information need to be displayed, but it should also be styled and formatted such that users can assess it at a glance.

Executive Summary

This topic examines what information to include in the list item info to strike the balance between providing sufficient information about each product without overloading users and slowing them down. This involves identifying the most vital attributes for each product type, and consistently showcasing information that helps the user better evaluate and compare the List Items.

During test sessions, subjects would end up overlooking or misinterpreting products when important attributes were not included in list items. Not only should key general attributes like price, user ratings averages, and the number of user ratings be provided, but so should category-specific attributes that are vital to users’ ability to differentiate between products. Likewise, the product status — such as whether the product is out of stock or available to ship — is of primary importance for users determining whether to investigate products further or to rule them out.

Presentation of list item info is also highly important. If individual elements of information are difficult to distinguish or understand, users could overlook key attributes and mistakenly dismiss suitable products. Furthermore, if attributes are only included for some products but not all, comparing similar items becomes needlessly difficult.

Along with list item thumbnails, list item info forms the basis for users’ decisions on the suitability of products. Missing or poorly presented list item info can result in users not only overlooking suitable items, but can also lead to users wasting time visiting multiple product pages due to incomplete or unclear list item info.

Guidelines

Always Show Prices in Product List Items And Search Results #452

When prices are missing, users will be unable to efficiently scan list items to evaluate the suitability of products and could discard them.

Always Include Category-Specific Product Attributes in Spec-Driven List Items #2281

Without sufficient and relevant list item information users are likely to discard perfectly suitable products.

If an Extended Delivery Date Isn’t Available, Always Inform Users in Product Lists And Search Results if Items Are “Out of Stock” #2369

Users can waste a lot of time if they have carefully considered items in product lists or search results only to find that they are “Out of Stock” on product pages.

Consider Providing an Extended Delivery Date in Product List Items And Search Results for Products Temporarily “Out of Stock” #528

Listing a product as “out of stock” when it’s simply temporarily unavailable could lead to abandonments as users search elsewhere to purchase the item.

Always Make Separate List Item Info Elements in Product Lists And Search Results Visually Distinct #448

If users can’t quickly and accurately discern key product attributes when scanning list item info in product lists and search results, they could overlook suitable items.

Always Include the User Ratings Average in List Items And Search Results #2284

Many users need to know how others feel about products as part of judging their suitability, but this information is sometimes missing from the product list and search results.

Always Show the Number of User Ratings in List Items #441

If the reliability and validity of user ratings averages is only revealed on product pages, product finding can be unnecessarily slowed.

Include Product Attributes Consistently Across List Items And Search Results of the Same Product Type #443

If users can’t compare similar items when in product lists and search results, they’ll have to detour to product pages to find missing information, or may miss out on suitable products.

Display “Price Per Unit” For Multiquantity Items #446

Without being able to compare prices accurately, users may miss out on the best prices or may reject good-value items.

Mobile List Item Info Should Highlight Products’ Key Features #932

Users rely heavily on text in product list items and search results to help them find suitable products, especially since thumbnails often provide insufficient visual details.

Consider Making Brands Easy to Identify in List Items And Search Results When They Are of High Importance #444

Brand-conscious users could miss out on suitable items if they can’t identify brands in product lists and search results.

Ensure Text in Mobile List Items and Search Results is Unselectable #1070

Making list item text selectable can cause users to detour to product pages and slows the process of scanning the product list.

Benchmark Analysis

Desktop Web

As one of the worst performing topics in Product Lists & Filtering, the average site performed “poorly” with 72% of sites performing at a level of “mediocre” or worse. When sites do not consistently provide relevant and useful list item information, users may struggle to determine which products are worth investigating further. They may subsequently overlook otherwise suitable products or spend excessive time and energy ‘pogo-sticking’ between prospective product pages and the product list.

Poor-performing sites neglected to include some basic list item information that would have otherwise been expected and could easily have been included:

  • 66% of sites did not display the price per unit (PPU) for multi-quantity items (#446).
  • 40% of sites included, at most, only a single piece of category-specific information within list items (#2281).
  • 57% of sites did not include the user ratings average in list items while of the sites that did, 65% of them got key implementation details wrong such as using colored stars to indicate the ratings average as opposed to numerals (#2284).

Beyond this, poor-performing sites made gleaning information from list items particularly challenging. For example, 58% of sites did not include product information consistently across products of the same product type (#443), while 34% of sites conglomerated product information into titles without any form of visual separation or styling to distinguish list item information elements from each other (#448). Furthermore, where out-of-stock products are concerned, 68% of sites did not clearly label them as such, nor allowed users to add them to the cart with an extended delivery date (#2369).

For inspiration, look to HP and Crutchfield for their state-of-the-art implementations within this topic.

Mobile Web

Similar to, yet marginally worse than its Desktop counterpart, the average site within the topic of Mobile: List Item Info performed “poorly” with an alarming 56% of sites performing at a level of “poor” or worse. Poor-performing List Items often lacked pertinent pieces of product information, which made the comparison of prospective products needlessly difficult and forced users to ‘pogo-stick’ between product pages and the product list to glean further information, more specifically:

  • 73% of sites did not include product information consistently across list items of the same product type, making it difficult to compare products without visiting multiple product pages (#443).

  • For instance, 45% of sites included, at most, only a single piece of category-specific information within list items (#2281), and 87% did not consistently display both the total price and the price per unit (PPU) for multi-quantity items (#446).

  • On top of this, of the sites that supported user ratings, 42% of them did not include the user ratings average in list items, and of the sites that did, 70% of them got key implementation details wrong such as using colored stars to indicate the ratings average as opposed to numerals (#2284).

Moreover, when product specifications and features were included within list items, they were often done so haphazardly, which made it difficult to discern and compare features across products, more specifically:

  • Instead of highlighting key product features, 46% of sites did the opposite by either truncating list item information or by not including brand information (#932).

  • 27% of sites included product information in titles without any form of visual separation or styling to distinguish list item information elements from each other (#448). For instance, 54% of sites could improve their list item titles by distinguishing brand information from other list item details (#444).

  • 68% of sites did not make list item text on list items unselectable, a snag that testing revealed to be a nuisance for users when they accidentally selected text while scanning list items (#1070).

For inspiration, see Crutchfield and HP for their “state-of-the-art” List Item Information implementations.
